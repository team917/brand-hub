04·Month-by-month

The twelve months, in full.

Every month has a working outcome and a signed deliverable. No months disappear into "strategy work."

Executive literacy. What AI can and can't do, honestly.

Two half-day workshops with your C-suite and department heads. Grounded case studies from analogous companies. Honest failure modes. We finish with your leadership team speaking the same language about AI and aligned on what "good" looks like.

What we do

  • Two executive workshops (half-day each)
  • Analogous case study briefing (your industry)
  • Common-failure walkthrough
  • Kick-off weekly office hours

You leave with

  • Shared vocabulary and success criteria
  • Named program sponsor + steering committee
  • Weekly office-hour cadence live
M 02 Phase 01 · Learn

Process mapping. Every workflow, every system, every dataset.

Interviews and shadow sessions across departments. We map the actual work, not the org chart. Data landscape and system inventory get built in parallel. This is the foundation everything else stands on.

What we do

  • 30-60 workflow interviews (per department scope)
  • Shadow sessions with front-line teams
  • Data inventory: what lives where, who owns it
  • System inventory: stack, integrations, tech debt

You leave with

  • Process atlas (every candidate workflow, documented)
  • Data map and ownership register
  • System diagram and integration surface
  • Named workflow owners per department
M 03 Phase 01 · Learn

Impact x Risk audit. Verdict on every workflow. Locked.

We score every candidate workflow on impact if it works and risk if AI gets it wrong. Verdicts assigned: automate hard, hybrid HITL, leave alone, or kill it. Top twelve workflows ranked for build order. You leave phase one with a signed twelve-month roadmap.

What we do

  • Score every workflow: impact and risk
  • Assign verdict on each: automate / hybrid / leave / kill
  • Rank top 12 build candidates
  • Present findings to executive committee

You leave with

07·Fit check

Who this is for.

You'll get a lot out of this if you fit.

  • You're a large company (typically 200+ employees, but not a hard rule)
  • You've watched competitors adopt AI and don't want to be behind again
  • An executive sponsor is willing to commit real time, not just a budget line
  • You'd rather ship four workflows than admire twenty PowerPoint slides
  • You want the people who built it to teach your team how to run it
  • You're okay with plain talk, not consulting theatre

Skip this if any of these are true.

  • You want a strategy deck and no execution
  • You want to buy licenses without building the workflows behind them
  • No executive is willing to be publicly accountable for AI outcomes
  • You're looking for "AI-washing" for investor narrative only
  • You need a name-brand consulting firm on the invoice for optics
  • Your organization can't tolerate any AI ever being wrong
08·FAQ

Straight answers.

How much does the program cost?

Depends on your size, scope, and how many departments the workflows touch. We price the audit as a fixed fee, then the build months as a monthly retainer with milestone deliverables. We are direct on cost early - no long dance. Book a conversation and we will give you a range in the first call.

Who actually shows up?

The people who will do the work. You get named workflow builders, a workflow lead, and Joe (as program lead) accountable end-to-end. No junior handoff mid-program. No mystery offshore team behind the scenes.

What tech do you build on?

We are tech-agnostic on principle. In practice: LLM APIs (Anthropic, OpenAI), orchestration (n8n, Zapier, or custom), your existing systems (Salesforce, HubSpot, Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, etc.), and whatever database and dashboard you already run. If you have a preferred stack we honor it. If you don't, we recommend and defend our choice.

How is this different from a Big Four AI practice?

Big Four sells you a beautifully-formatted strategy and a matrixed team. The strategy is often good. The execution rarely arrives on time or works the way the strategy claimed. We only sell working systems that our team stays with through deployment. Fewer slides, more shipped workflows.

What if we already have AI initiatives running?

Then we do not overwrite them. Month two's process mapping includes everything you have already built. If your existing initiatives are working, we help scale them. If they are stuck, we help unstick them. Sometimes the honest answer is: keep going, we are not adding value. We will say that.

Can we start with the six-month version and extend?

Yes. That's how many clients start. The six-month program is designed to give you a fully defensible answer on whether to extend, without you having committed to the full year upfront.

How do you measure success?

We agree on specific, boring metrics before we start. Hours returned per team per week. Dollars saved per workflow per month. Customer NPS delta on any customer-facing workflow. Workflow uptime. Adoption rate on the pilot teams. If we cannot measure it, we cannot claim it.

Will you sign an NDA and handle sensitive data?

Yes on NDA. Yes on standard enterprise data-handling: SOC-adjacent controls, PII redaction where appropriate, access logs on our end, and vendor security review is welcomed. We do not train models on your data unless you specifically ask us to.
